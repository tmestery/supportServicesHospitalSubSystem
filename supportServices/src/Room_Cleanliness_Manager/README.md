### **Use Case: Process Room & Bed Cleanliness Turnover**

**Trigger:**
Patient discharge or scheduled room cleaning.

**Actor:**
Cleaning Staff

**Preconditions:**

* Cleaning staff is logged into the hospital management system (if applicable).
* Staff has access to cleaning supplies, equipment, and personal protective equipment (PPE).
* The room is ready to be cleaned (e.g., the patient has been discharged or temporarily moved).

**Main Success Scenario (Basic Flow):**

1. Cleaning staff checks the room assignment and cleaning schedule.
2. Removes used linens, disposes of waste, and sanitizes surfaces according to protocol.
3. Cleans and disinfects the bed and surrounding area.
4. Replaces linens and ensures all supplies and equipment are in proper order.
5. Updates the hospital system (or logbook) confirming the room and bed are clean and ready for the next patient.

**Outcome:**

* The room and bed are properly cleaned and sanitized.
* Room status in the system reflects readiness for the next patient.
* Hospital hygiene and patient safety standards are maintained.
